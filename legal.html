Legal Information

Quick Checklist

There's a lot to the legalities of riding a PEV in Brisbane, but here's a quick checklist to keep in mind that applies everywhere in Queensland based on Transport and Main Roads guidelines.

Do

  • check Wear an approved Bicycle Helmet while riding.
  • check Ride on footpaths. Keep left of oncoming traffic.
  • check Have a white front light + rear red light.
  • check Ride on local roads. Roads without centre dividing lines that are under 50km/h.
  • checkOnly use the bicycle side of a shared path.

Don't

  • block Ride on Main roads. You can for up to 50m to avoid an obstacle.
  • blockGo above 25km/h.
  • blockDrink and drive (alcohol).
  • blockCarry passengers.
  • blockUse your mobile phone while riding.

It's well worth printing out a copy of TMR's rules which are available at this link: https://www.qld.gov.au/transport/safety/rules/wheeled-devices/personal-mobility-devices

Local Council/Government Rules

On top of the TMR Rules there are also specific stipulations by some Local Governments, listed below.

Brisbane City Council

Brisbane City Council publish their guidelines online. The quick takeaways to add on top are below:
  • check You can legally ride on bikeways, shared paths and footpaths.
  • block_box Don't ride in public squares or malls, where signs prohibiting their use are displayed.

Ipswich City Council

Ipswich City Council do not post their rules online in an any way that could be found, though their Customer Support team advises that they do not have any specific rulings around PEV use in their jurisdiction.
